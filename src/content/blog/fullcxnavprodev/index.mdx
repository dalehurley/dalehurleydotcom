Ever faced a project where "a decision based on the data" was the only direction provided? It's like embarking on a treasure hunt without a map. Precise requirements are not just helpful, they're absolutely crucial for our success.

When the brief is too broad, the lack of specificity results in delivery teams entering a constant loop of guessing games—far from ideal.

**The Power of a Good Requirement:**
Good requirements are the GPS for project navigation. They tell us where we're going, the best route to get there, and when we've arrived. They are the guardrails that keep our project on track, ensuring that everyone is headed in the same direction.

**What Delivery Teams Need:**
Delivery teams thrive on details. What is the decisioning engine supposed to decide? What data is it using? What does success look like? These aren't just helpful hints; they're the building blocks for construction.

**Avoiding the Pitfalls of Ambiguity:**
Staying clear of the ambiguity trap means taking the time to define the project's scope. It's a collaborative effort that requires asking the right questions and nailing down the answers before diving in.
